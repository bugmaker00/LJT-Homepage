This paper presents a novel approach to hallucination mitigation in large language models. By analyzing the inner representation dynamics during in-context learning, we discover that sharpness in representations can serve as an early warning signal for hallucinations. Our method provides a new perspective on understanding and reducing hallucination in LLMs.
